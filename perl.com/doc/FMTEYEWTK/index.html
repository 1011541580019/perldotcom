Far More Than Everything You've Ever Wanted to Know About...

This series contains a set of documents that address perl-related certain topic areas in depth. They range from nothing but simple copies of somewhat lengthy news postings to real, full-blown, htmlified, complete tutorials. Don't neglect the faq or manual, though.

HTML format

Here's what I have in HTML format:
  • My style talk from the 1998 Perl Conference.
  • Gisle Aas's wonderful illustrated guide to Perl's internals.
  • A tutorial on Perl objects. For general information on objects, you might also want to check out The OO Soapbox.
  • An explanation of how pseudorandom numbers work by Tom Phoenix.
  • An explanation of why Perl "lets" you delete or rename read-only files.
  • Is Perl a Compiler or an Interpreter?
  • Bill Middleton's perl4 to perl5 traps page.
  • What Makes Perl's Regular Expressions Dynamite
  • Backtracking in regular expressions
  • Perl Data Structures Cookbook (perldsc etc man pages broken up by section)
  • Interprocess Communication (perlipc man page broken up by section)
  • Using tie() (perltie man page broken up by section)
  • How to know whether something's numeric
  • Lots of details on sorting
  • An easy introduction to perl5 objects.
