On the day after the Perl Conference,O'Reilly & Associates is organizing the first Open Source Developer's Day in San Jose. The Day is intended for developers who are interested in becoming involved in open source development projects or in learning more about how projects like Linux and Perl work.

The Developer's Day will explore Open Source development from a variety of perspectives, including project management, commercial business models, and legal and licensing issues. Eric S. Raymond will give the opening keynote and the featured presenters include:

  • Brian Behlendorf, Apache Group
  • Richard Stallman, Free Software Foundation
  • Pam Samuelson, University of California at Berkeley Center for Law and Technology
  • John Ousterhout, TCL
  • Larry Wall, Perl
  • Bruce Parens, Debian Linux
In addition, representatives from Netscape, IBM, and Red Hat Linux will be participating.

Admission to Developer's Day on July 21, 1998 is $295 and attendees of the Perl Conference will receive a $100 discount.

At 5 pm that evening, O'Reilly will also be hosting The Open Source Town Meeting, a wide-open discussion on Open Source developments. This event has a $10 admission charge with proceeds going to the Free Software Foundation.

For more information on Open Source Developer's Day and the Open Source Town Hall Meeting, visit the O'Reilly Open Source web site.
